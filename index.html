World War II (1939-1945) was a global conflict involving the Allies (mainly the US, Soviet Union, UK,
China, and France) against the Axis (Germany, Italy, and Japan). Key causes included the Treaty of
Versailles, expansionist policies of the Axis powers, and the failure of appeasement.
Major events:
1. 1939: Germany invaded Poland, starting the war.
2. 1940: Fall of France.
3. 1941: Germany invaded the Soviet Union; Japan attacked Pearl Harbor.
4. 1944: D-Day, Allied forces landed in Normandy.
5. 1945: Germany and Japan surrendered after the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
The war resulted in an estimated 70-85 million deaths, the Holocaust, the formation of the United
Nations, and the beginning of the Cold War.
